What is porousRTFoam?

porousRTFoam is a package for solving coupled reactive transport processes in porous media using OpenFOAM®, a popular open-source software for CFD simulation. We implement the micro-continuum approach, which enable the investigation of coupled biogeochemical processes occurring at both pore scale and Darcy scale. This package is able to record porosity evolution during reactive transport in porous media, including flow and solute transport, mineral dissolution and precipitation, biofilm growth and bioclogging, and microbially induced calcite carbonate precipitation. porousRTFoam facilitates the coupling of biogeochemical properties in typical porous and fractured media, offering a wide range of application opportunities from CCUS to contaminant remediation.

About porousRTFoam

The governing equations of porousRTFoam is based on the Darcy-Brinkman-Stokes equation.

Solvers

The porousRTFoam is consist of dbsFoam, dbscFoam, dissolFoam, precipFoam, bioFoam, and micpFoam.

dbsFoam

The solver is developed to solve porous media flow using the Darcy-Brinkman-Stokes equation.

dbscFoam

The solver is developed to solve solute transport in porous media using the Darcy-Brinkman-Stokes equation and the Advection-Diffusion equation.

dissolFoam

The solver is developed to solve the coupled process of calcite carbonate dissolution and solute transport in porous media using the Darcy-Brinkman-Stokes equation.

precipFoam

The solver is developed to solve the coupled process of calcite carbonate precipitation and solute transport in porous media using the Darcy-Brinkman-Stokes equation.

bioFoam

The solver is developed to solve the coupled process of biofilm growth and solute transport in porous media using the Darcy-Brinkman-Stokes equation and the Monod kinetics.

micpFoam

The solver is developed to solve the coupled process of microbially induced calcite carbonate precipitation and solute transport in porous media using the Darcy-Brinkman-Stokes equation and the Michaelis‐Menten kinetics.
